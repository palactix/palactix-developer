# Bring Your Own OAuth (BYO)

Palactix follows a **Bring Your Own OAuth (BYO)** model.

This means you use your own platform developer apps instead of relying on shared credentials.

---

## What BYO OAuth Means

With Palactix:

- You create apps on each platform (Meta, LinkedIn, etc.)  
- You provide your own `client_id` and `client_secret`  
- OAuth tokens are issued to your app, not Palactix  

---

## Why This Matters

### Ownership

- You own your client connections  
- Tokens belong to your infrastructure  
- You can move away from Palactix anytime  

---

### Branding

- Users see your app name during OAuth  
- Not a third-party tool  
- Builds trust with clients  

---

### Isolation

- Your rate limits are not shared  
- Your app reputation is independent  
- Other users cannot affect your account  

---

## Traditional Model (Without BYO)

Most tools use shared OAuth apps:

- All users connect through the same app  
- Tokens are controlled by the vendor  
- Rate limits are shared  
- High vendor lock-in  

---

## Palactix Model (BYO)

With Palactix:

- Each developer uses their own apps  
- Tokens are stored securely per app  
- Full control over credentials  
- No shared dependency  

---

## How It Works in Palactix

1. Create a developer app in Palactix  
2. Add platform credentials (client_id, client_secret)  
3. Generate access token  
4. Connect user accounts via OAuth  

---

## Example

During OAuth:

```bash
User sees:
"Continue with Your App Name"
```

The user authorizes **your app**, not Palactix.

---

## When to Use BYO OAuth

BYO OAuth is ideal if you:

- Build SaaS products for clients  
- Manage multiple client accounts  
- Need long-term ownership of data  
- Want to avoid platform dependency  

---

## Summary

BYO OAuth ensures that:

- You own the relationship  
- You control the credentials  
- You are not locked into a vendor  

Palactix provides the infrastructure, while you retain control.
